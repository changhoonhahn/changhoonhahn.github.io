Research

My current research interests include...

Galaxy Evolution

One of my main research interests is in galaxy formation and evolution. One aspect of galaxy evolution that I've been interested in, is the cessation of star formation in galaxies. For my first project in grad school, I used galaxies from the PRIsm MUlit-object Survey to study the effect of galaxy environment on the quiescent fraction evolution [ADS]. Lately, I've been investigating the star formation quenching timescale of central galaxies within the context of hierarchical galaxy formation.

Large Scale Structure

Another one of my main research interests is in observational cosmology. More specifically, I'm interested in large scale structure and galaxy clustering. For some time now I've been measuring and analyzing the galaxy powerspectrum and bispectrum using SDSS-III BOSS galaxies. Recently, in order to improve redshift space distortion analyses, I've been studying the impact of systematic effects such as fiber collisions on the galaxy powerspectrum.

Statistical Methods

I am also interested statistical challenges in astronomy and cosmology. Recently, I've been exploring likehood free inference methods such as Approximate Bayesian Computation for parameter inference.

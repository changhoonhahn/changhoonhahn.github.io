Research

large scale structure

The connection between galaxies and the underlying matter distribution in the Universe allows us to use them to make precise measurements of large scale structure. Galaxy surveys like SDSS-III BOSS are mapping out the Universe with over a million galaxy redshifts for this purpose. BOSS (and future surveys like eBOSS, DESI, and PFS) use fiber-fed spectrographs, which due to physical constraints on the focal plane, fail to collect redshifts from a fraction of galaxies that are too close to each other. This systematic effect significantly impacts galaxy clustering measurements and ultimately the cosmological parameter constraints. In Hahn et al.(2016), I developed different methods to account for this "fiber collision" for galaxy power spectrum analysis. I'm now working to extend this for the galaxy bispectrum.

galaxy evolution

One aspect of galaxy evolution that I've been particularly interested in is the cessation of star formation ("star formation quenching") in galaxies. In Hahn et al.(2015), I used galaxies from the PRIsm MUlit-object Survey (PRIMUS) to examine how galaxy environment affects star formation quenching. More recently, in Hahn et al.(2016), I used models that combine observations of the galaxy population with a high resolution cosmological N-body simulation to measure the timescale of star formation quenching for central galaxies (poster).

statistical methods
